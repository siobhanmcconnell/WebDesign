The Three Musketeers

By Alexandre Dumas

"In which it is established that, despite their names ending in os and is, there is nothing mythological about the heroes of the story I am going to have the honor of telling my readers."

The Three Musketeers

Chapter 1

The Three Gifts of Monsieur d'Artagnan the Elder

Our protagonist, D'Artagnan, sets off for Paris. His father gives him three gifts to aid his journey. One of these gifts includes an introduction letter to Monsieur de Treville, the captain of the King's Musketeers.

Chapter 2

Monsieur de Treville's Anteroom

Upon waiting for his audience with Monsieur de Treville, D'Artagnan comes across the titular musketeers, Athos, Porthos, and Aramis.

Chapter 3

The Audience

D'Artagnan explains his situation to Monsieur de Treville, and it quickly comes apparent that D'artagnan has accidentally stumbled upon a complex plot reaching far above his station.
